Welcome

My mission is to deliver better data to more people, to enable insightful decisions. I've carried this theme through a PhD in Applied Physics at Harvard, several collaborations between chefs and scientists, and my curret job at Fitbit.

I apply this philosophy to my own life, through the way I look at my own Fitbit data (example), experiment with task-management systems, and refine my note-taking techniques. I draw inspiration from numerous sources: the data-filled personal dashboards of Nicholas Felton, the elegant Bullet Journal method of Ryder Carroll, and the fascinating explorations of human nature by Christian Rudder.

Disclaimer: although I spend my days analyzing data at Fitbit, all the views here are my own.

 Tryo

A tryo is a one- to four-week long trial of a new habit to see if it's a good fit. You can pick whichever analogy appeals to you more:

  • Your daily routine is like the master branch of a git repository. Each time you change some part of that is a routine. Some are bad ideas that are stashed away; others are successful and get pulled back into the main branch.
  • Creating your daily routine is like datingdifferent habits . It's difficult to try too many habits at once and some habits just aren't meant for you. However, even if you don't end up incorporating a habit for life, it can change the way you think about the world.

 Kitchen Experiments

Since I no longer work in a science lab, my kitchen has become my venue for experimentation. I'm especially interested in recipes that challenge assumptions with their simplicity (i.e. pea soup = pea + water + salt) or their unusual use of ingredients (e.g. muffin = almond flour + egg).
